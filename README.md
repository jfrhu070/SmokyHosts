This is important to understand: SmokyHosts positions itself as an unmanaged hosting provider. If you need hand-holding for every WordPress plugin installation or expect someone to troubleshoot your custom application code, this might not be your match. They handle the infrastructure—servers, networks, uptime—but expect customers to manage their own software stack.

The mixed reviews suggest that experienced users who understand server management tend to have positive experiences, appreciating the customization options, pricing, and stability. Less technical users or those expecting managed hosting support may find the experience frustrating.

Data center locations mentioned include Buffalo (New York) and Los Angeles for their U.S. presence, with infrastructure partnerships through Hivelocity, OVH, Colocrossing, and other providers. Multiple reviewers mentioned good network connectivity and speeds, though some noted data transfer speeds were "slower than expected with a 10 mbit connection" but "still good enough."

The affiliate program appears legitimate, offering 15% recurring commission with a $10 signup bonus and 90-day cookie tracking. Several entrepreneurs in the reseller community have mentioned using SmokyHosts as their backend provider while building their own hosting brands.

Bottom line from the reviews: if you're technically competent, want customization, and don't need managed support, SmokyHosts delivers solid uptime and value. If you're looking for white-glove service where support walks you through every technical decision, you might want to look elsewhere or be prepared for self-learning.
